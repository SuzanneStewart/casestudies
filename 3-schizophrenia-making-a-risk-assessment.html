3 Schizophrenia -- Making a risk assessment

Now we're going to look at the risk assessment aspect. PLEASE NOTE that this section includes questions relating to self-harm and suicidal intent.

  • Keep taking notes of

    • EVIDENCE (i.e., what does the client say or describe)

    • SYMPTOMS and FACTORS (i.e., from a practitioner perspectve, what symptom or relevant factor does that evidence demonstrate for example, worry, hallucinations, stressors, etc.)

    • TIMINGS (i.e., durations of experiences; frequency of experiences)

    • IMPACT and FUNCTIONING (i.e., what negative effects on the client's life have occurred as a result of the experiences)

  • In particular, note how the practitioner approaches this sensitive part of the assessment.
